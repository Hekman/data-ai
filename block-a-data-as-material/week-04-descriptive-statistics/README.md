# Block A · Week 4 — Descriptive Statistics

> **Block question:** *What does this data actually let me know, and what stories can I tell?*
> **This week's question:** *How do I capture a dataset in a few numbers without distorting it?*

---

Descriptive statistics are the vocabulary for saying what a dataset is like: where it centres, how it spreads, how variables move together. This week is about interpretation, not formulas. Knowing which summary fits which question, and spotting when a tidy number conceals a messy reality. A single statistic often becomes the headline of a data story; statistical literacy is what lets you choose an honest one.

**Why this matters for designers**

A single statistic often becomes the headline of a data story. 'Average response time fell 20%' is a design decision about what to foreground. If the mean is dragged by outliers, or the groups behind it diverge, your headline misleads even if the arithmetic is correct. Statistical literacy is what lets you choose an honest insight.

The slides for this class will be available here after class.

**By the end of this week you can:**

- Compute and interpret centre (mean/median/mode) and spread (range/IQR/SD).
- Choose mean vs median appropriately and read a correlation.
- Identify where a summary misleads (skew, outliers, hidden subgroups).
