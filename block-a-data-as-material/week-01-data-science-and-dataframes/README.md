# Block A · Week 1 — Data Science & Dataframes

> **Block question:** *What does this data actually let me know, and what stories can I tell?*
> **This week's question:** *What is data science, and how do I shape a table to answer a question?*

---

Welcome to the Data & AI track. This block treats data as a designer's material: something you collect, question, shape, and compose with, not just consume. This first week moves fast on purpose: a shared map of the data science workflow, a working notebook, and straight into the core material of the block: the DataFrame and the instructions that shape it (select, filter, sort, group, aggregate).

The skill isn't memorising Pandas syntax; it's learning to translate a question into a sequence of table operations — and to read what a dataset does and does not contain before you trust it. Aggregation is where the interesting design decisions hide: what you choose to group by determines the story the data can tell.

** 
Why this matters for designers**

Most people consume dashboards and charts as finished objects. As a data-driven designer your job starts one step earlier: at the raw material. Every chosen column, every filter, every aggregation is a design decision that shapes what someone can know. What you choose to group by determines the story the data can tell.

**By the end of this week you can:**

- Name the steps of the data science workflow and explain the purpose of each.
- Load a dataset into a pandas DataFrame and articulate what it does and does not contain.
- Select, filter, sort, and use groupby + aggregation at a chosen granularity.
