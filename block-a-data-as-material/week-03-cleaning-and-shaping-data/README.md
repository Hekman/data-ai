# Block A · Week 3 — Cleaning & Shaping Data

> **Block question:** *What does this data actually let me know, and what stories can I tell?*
> **This week's question:** *Real data is messy! what do I fix, what do I drop, what do I leave, and how do I stay honest about it?*

---

Real datasets sometimes arrive broken: wrong types, missing values, duplicates, impossible entries. This week is about diagnosing and repairing them while staying aware that every fix is a decision that changes what the data can say. The deliverable habit this week is the cleaning log: one line per transformation, so your work stays reproducible and defensible.

**Why this matters for designers**

A chart built on silently mishandled data is a beautiful lie. The credibility of everything you design downstream rests on choices made here. Professionals are distinguished not by clean data but by defensible cleaning: being able to say exactly what they did to a missing value and why.

** 
By the end of this week you can:**

- Diagnose common data-quality problems (types, missing values, duplicates, impossible values).
- Choose between drop / impute / flag and justify the choice.
- Reshape data wide↔long, and keep a cleaning log.
